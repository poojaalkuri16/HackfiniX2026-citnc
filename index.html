About Hackfinity

Hackfinity 2026 is a 24-hour national-level hackathon that brings together students, developers, designers, and innovators from across the country. Participants collaborate, build solutions, and push the boundaries of creativity and technology.

Hosted by Techfinity Club at Cambridge Institute of Technology North Campus, this event fosters innovation, teamwork, and real-world problem solving.

FAQs

Got questions about Hackfinity? We’ve got you covered. Explore everything you need to know—from registration and submissions to rules, rewards, and event details.

What is Hackfinity?

Hackfinity is a 24-hour hackathon designed to foster innovation and creativity by challenging participants to solve real-world problems aligned with Industry 5.0. It brings together students to collaborate, build impactful solutions, and showcase their technical and problem-solving skills.

Participants can explore the detailed problem statements in the Problem Statements section.

Who can participate in Hackfinity?

Open to undergraduate students pursuing BE/BTech, BCA/MCA, BSc (CS), and other relevant degrees.

What is the team size allowed for participation?

Each team must consist of 3 to 4 members. Teams can be formed across different departments and semesters, encouraging diverse perspectives and collaborative problem-solving.

What is the last date for team registration and PPT submission?

The last date for team registration and submission of the PPT (in PDF format) is 22nd April 2026.

What is the selection/shortlisting process?

Teams will be shortlisted based on the evaluation of their submitted presentation (PPT), which must be uploaded in PDF format. The file should be named in the format: team_name.pdf. Submissions will be assessed on the basis of idea clarity, innovation, and relevance to the given problem statements.

Is there any registration fee for Hackfinity?

Shortlisted teams are required to pay a registration fee of ₹100 per participant.

What should the PPT include?

Participants are required to follow the official PPT template provided by the organizers. The template, along with the rulebook and brochure, is available on our website and Unstop under the “Download Attachments” section. Teams are advised to adhere to the given format and guidelines while preparing their submission.

What are the evaluation criteria for shortlisting/final judging?

Projects will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

  • Innovation & Creativity
  • Technical Implementation
  • Functionality & Feasibility
  • Impact & Relevance
  • Presentation & Demonstration

The decision of the judging panel will be final and binding.

Will participants receive certificates or rewards?

Yes, Hackfinity offers an exciting prize pool worth ₹2.6 lakh, along with additional rewards and gifts. All participants will also receive certificates of participation.

Where will Hackfinity be conducted?

Hackfinity will be held at Cambridge Institute of Technology North Campus, Bangalore.

Will accommodation and food be provided?

Yes, accommodation and food will be provided for all participants throughout the duration of the hackathon.

Whom should participants contact for queries or support?

For any queries or support, participants can find the relevant contact details at the end of our homepage on the official website, as well as on our Unstop page. Feel free to reach out to us for assistance.
